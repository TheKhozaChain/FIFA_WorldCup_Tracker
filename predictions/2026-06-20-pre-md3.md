# Model face-off — predictions locked in before Matchday 3

**Locked in: 2026-06-20**, with the group stage at **32 of 72 matches played**
(Matchdays 1–2 complete in some groups, MD1 in others). Matchday 3 — the final
round of group games — begins **24 June 2026**. These numbers were committed to
git *before* any MD3 result, so the commit timestamp is the proof that neither
model was tuned after the fact.

Two independent forecasts of each team's probability of reaching the **Round of
32** (top 2 per group + 8 best third-placed teams):

- **Model A — Monte Carlo** (`wctracker`, this repo): simulates every remaining
  fixture 50,000× from Elo-seeded Poisson scorelines, resolving the real WC2026
  rules (head-to-head before goal difference; 8 best thirds). Output, not
  judgement.
- **Model B — Heuristic** (hand-maintained by a friend): de-biased
  favourite–longshot baselines, nudged per result and renormalised to 3,200%.

Both columns independently sum to **3,200%** (exactly 32 teams advance).

## How this gets scored (fill in after MD3)

When the group stage finishes, set **Actual = 100** if the team reached the
Round of 32, else **0**. Then for each model, **error = |prediction − Actual|**.
Lower mean error wins. The decided teams (Mexico, Haiti, …) score ~0 for both,
so the **contested** teams below are what actually settle it.
